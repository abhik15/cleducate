Launch of Indus World School

At Indus World School we believe in learning by doing. We believe that education cannot be limited to the four walls of the classroom, and it is because of this philosophy that everything under the sun is part of the curriculum at Indus.

Our philosophy was put into action at the launch of the Indus World School in Indore. Students got an opportunity to test this philosophy in the company of flute maestro Pandit Hariprasad Chaurasia at Sayaji Mahal on January 9 2005. In the program organized by the school, students performed in the company of the flute maestro, and experienced the joy of learning by doing.

Pt. Hariprasad Chaurasia led the students in a program, where the performances by the students were as bewitching as those by the maestro himself. Pt. Hariprasad Chaurasia’s performance was marked by a range of complex and simple tunes, the students paid homage to the maestro by showcasing their talent through folk and devotional tunes. Performances by the students, Sameer Rao, Parag Chhabbra, Nivedita Pandya and Srichandra Aggrawal, ranged from soulful renditions of folk songs to lively patriotic tunes.

The program was very successful in showcasing the philosophy of the Indus World School, as well as giving the students and the parents a chance to experience success in “careers that might be”.

After the performances, the school’s Chairman, Sujit Bhattacharyya spoke about the school’s purpose and aim, and felicitated the people associated with the school. The program concluded with a vote of thanks by R Sreenivasan, Chairman, Indus World School, Hyderabad and Deepak Pillai, Project Manager, Indus World School, Indore.
