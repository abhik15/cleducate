Inquiry-based Learning

Learning can never happen in isolation. Children are used to learning new things based on their ability to ‘build up’ on previously existing knowledge. The theory of constructivism, one of the most profound learnings in the field of ‘how children learn’ tells us that. Inquiry based learning follows this simple understanding. The more you ask, the more you learn.

The art of questioning
It is of prime importance for every Indus mentor to have mastered. It is no simple task to ask the right questions. Ones that give rise to further questioning.

The art of answering
Equally challenging is the art of answering a question in a way that does not give a full and final answer to the previous one, rather stimulates the learner to think beyond and ask more questions. It has a lot to do with making your own co-relations in mind and reaching your own conclusions.
